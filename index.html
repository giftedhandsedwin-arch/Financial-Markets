The Origin of Financial Markets

The roots of modern financial markets date back to the 11th century, but the first recognizable stock exchange was established in 1602 by the Dutch East India Company (VOC) in Amsterdam. Before this, "brokers" met in coffee houses or town squares to trade debts and commodities. The 17th-century Amsterdam exchange introduced the ability for the general public to buy shares and receive dividends, effectively bircing the concept of public equity.
